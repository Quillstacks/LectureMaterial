\section{The Why}\index{assessment}\index{project report}

A lecture that teaches tools and workflows must also assess tools and workflows.
Written exams test recall; a project report tests whether you can actually build 
and deploy something.
The Projektbericht is your proof that you have walked the path from empty 
virtual machines
to a running, deployed application.

\marginnote{\newthought{The Projektbericht} replaces a traditional exam.
You submit a written report documenting your decisions, your architecture, 
your modular extension,
and the problems you solved along the way.}

\newthought{Pick any block from the course} and build a meaningful extension onto it.
The extension should add functionality, improve reliability, or strengthen security
in a way that goes beyond what the lecture covered.
The choice is yours, but each extension must be unique across the class:
submit your proposal for approval on a first come, first served basis with me.
Once an extension is taken, no other student may choose the same one.

\marginnote{\newthought{Nudge} replicate all course VM snapshots
for Apple Silicon using UTM, so future students on ARM Macs can follow along natively.
This means at least 3 Blocks per student, that benefits every cohort after yours.}

\newthought{Submit a written report as PDF.} Include your name, matriculation number,
and a link to your snapshot. The report must cover:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Which block you chose and what it covers in the course.
    \item What you built on top of it, which tools and techstack you used, and why it adds value.
    \item Problems you encountered and how you resolved them.
    \item What works, what remains incomplete, and what would you do next.
\end{itemize}

\newthought{Evaluation} considers three dimensions:
\emph{depth} of the extension beyond the lecture material,
\emph{understanding} demonstrated through explanation and a VM snapshot,
and \emph{quality} of the writing.
